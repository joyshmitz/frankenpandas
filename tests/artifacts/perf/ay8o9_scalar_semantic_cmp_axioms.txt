Scalar::semantic_cmp (fp-types) is the FOUNDATION of all ordering in the
library: sort_values, min/max, is_monotonic, searchsorted, groupby key order,
and the reference that every differential harness (59dk2/yfyb9/q70pn/2bgtq/k3zcv/
ya1po/5foyp/kaj3t) relies on (semantic_le/semantic_ge delegate to it). It had no
property test of its order axioms.

Harness (test fp-types: semantic_cmp_total_order_axioms_ay8o9): deterministic
seeded LCG (no rand crate, no mocks). For 6000 iterations over random SAME-dtype
finite/non-NaT scalars (Int64, finite Float64 in -5.0..=5.0, Utf8, Bool) it
asserts the total-order axioms:
  - reflexivity:   cmp(a,a) == Equal
  - antisymmetry:  cmp(a,b) == cmp(b,a).reverse()
  - transitivity:  a<=b && b<=c  =>  a<=c
  - le/ge/eq consistency: semantic_le(a,b)==(cmp!=Greater),
    semantic_ge(a,b)==(cmp!=Less), (le && ge)==(cmp==Equal)

It then PINS the intentional NaN degeneracy: a Float64 NaN compares Equal to
every finite Float64 and to itself (semantic_cmp uses partial_cmp().unwrap_or(
Equal)). This is exactly why ordering ops must treat NaN/NaT as MISSING rather
than relying on semantic_cmp to order it — and why the value-side differential
harnesses defer Float64/NaN to the Scalar path.

Result: PASS (1 passed; 6000 iterations + NaN pinning). The foundational
comparison is a sound total order over finite same-dtype values, with documented
NaN degeneracy.
